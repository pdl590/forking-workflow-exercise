# Brazilian feijoada recipe

Serve alongside [farofa](../sides/farofa.md) and [braised kale](../sides/braised_kale.md).

Feijoada is pronounced `/fej.ʒuˈa.dɐ/`.

## Ingredients

- **400 g** black beans (dried or 2 cans, drained and rinsed)
- **200 g** smoked sausage, sliced
- **150 g** bacon, chopped
- **300 g** pork shoulder or beef chunks (optional, for a simpler version you can omit)
- **1** medium onion, chopped
- **3 cloves** garlic, minced
- **2** bay leaves
- **1 tablespoon** butter
- **Salt and pepper** to taste
- **1 liter** water or broth
- **Cooked white rice** for serving
- **Orange slices** for serving (optional)

## Instructions

1. **Prepare Beans:**
   - If using dried beans, soak them overnight, then drain.
   - Add beans to a pot, cover with water, and simmer for about 45-60 minutes until tender. If using canned beans, skip this step.

2. **Cook Meats:**
   - Heat butter in a large pot over medium heat.
   - Add bacon and cook until it starts to crisp.
   - Add sausage and (if using) pork or beef, browning on all sides.

3. **Build Flavor:**
   - Add onion and garlic to the pot, cooking until softened.
   - Stir in bay leaves, salt, and pepper.

4. **Combine:**
   - Add the cooked (or canned) beans to the pot along with water or broth.
   - Stir well and bring to a gentle simmer.

5. **Simmer:**
   - Let the feijoada cook uncovered for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thickened and flavorful.

6. **Serve:**
   - Remove bay leaves before serving.
   - Serve hot with white rice and (optionally) orange slices on the side.
   - Best served with [farofa](../sides/farofa.md) and [braised kale](../sides/braised_kale.md) on the side.
