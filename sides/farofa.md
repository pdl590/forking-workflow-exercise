# Farofa

Serve alongside [feijoada](../mains/traditional-feijoada.md), [braised kale](../sides/braised_kale.md) and white rice.

Farofa is pronounced `/faˈɾɔ.fɐ/`.

## Ingredients
- **2 cups** cassava flour (farinha de mandioca)
- **3 eggs**
- **80 g** butter (or 60 ml neutral oil)
- **1 small onion**, finely chopped
- **2 cloves** garlic, minced
- **3 tablespoons** fresh chives, finely chopped
- **3 tablespoons** fresh parsley, finely chopped
- **Salt and black pepper** to taste

## Instructions
1. **Sauté aromatics:**
   - Heat butter in a large skillet over medium heat.
   - Add onion and cook until soft and translucent.
   - Add garlic and cook for about 30 seconds.

2. **Cook eggs:**
   - Push the aromatics to one side of the pan.
   - Crack the eggs into the empty side and scramble gently until just set.

3. **Add flour:**
   - Lower the heat slightly.
   - Add cassava flour gradually, stirring constantly to coat it in the fat and combine with the eggs.

4. **Toast:**
   - Continue stirring for 5-8 minutes until the farofa is lightly toasted and crumbly.

5. **Finish:**
   - Add chives and parsley.
   - Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
   - Mix well and serve warm.

## Notes
- Texture should be dry and crumbly, not greasy.
- Adjust butter or oil depending on how moist you prefer the farofa.
